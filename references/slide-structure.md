# Slide Structure — The 6-Slide Formula

## Overview
Every TikTok slideshow follows this 6-slide structure. Each slide has a specific job.

## The Formula

### Slide 1: THE HOOK (Scroll-Stopper)
- **Job:** Stop the scroll. Make them curious enough to swipe.
- **Text:** 4-6 words, reaction-style ("Wait... is this real??")
- **Image:** The most eye-catching shot. Sets the scene.
- **Rules:** Never use labels ("Modern Kitchen"). Always use reactions ("My landlord is gonna kill me")

### Slide 2: THE SETUP
- **Job:** Build context. Show the "before" or establish the situation.
- **Text:** Continue the story ("She said I can't change anything")
- **Image:** Ground the viewer in reality.

### Slide 3: THE TURN
- **Job:** Introduce the solution/tool/app. This is where the magic starts.
- **Text:** "So I downloaded this app..." or "Then I found..."
- **Image:** Transition moment.

### Slide 4: THE REVEAL (Part 1)
- **Job:** Show the first impressive result.
- **Text:** React to it ("Wait... is this actually the same place??")
- **Image:** First wow moment.

### Slide 5: THE REVEAL (Part 2)
- **Job:** Show another result. Build on the wow factor.
- **Text:** Escalate ("Okay I'm literally obsessed")
- **Image:** Different angle or variation of the result.

### Slide 6: THE CTA
- **Job:** Tell them what to do. Drive the action.
- **Text:** "[App Name] showed me what's possible — link in bio"
- **Image:** Best result shot. Leave them wanting more.

## Hook Categories

### 1. Person + Conflict
"My boyfriend said our flat looks like a catalogue"
"My landlord said I can't change anything"
"My mom thinks AI is taking over"

### 2. POV (Point of View)
"POV: You just discovered this app"
"POV: Your kitchen actually looks good for once"

### 3. Listicle
"5 rooms AI transformed in 30 seconds"
"3 things this app does that shouldn't be free"

### 4. Before/After
"Before vs after AI redesign"
"Same room, 30 seconds apart"

### 5. Tutorial/How-To
"How to redesign your room in 30 seconds"
"Step by step: AI room makeover"

### 6. Mistakes/Warnings
"Stop decorating your room like this"
"3 design mistakes everyone makes"

## Text Overlay Rules

1. **4-6 words per line** — short lines scannable at a glance
2. **Use \n for line breaks** — control the rhythm
3. **3-4 lines per slide** — more is fine, won't overflow
4. **REACTIONS not labels** — "Wait this is nice??" not "Modern style"
5. **No emoji** — canvas can't render them reliably
6. **Safe zones:** No text in bottom 20% (TikTok controls) or top 10% (status bar)

## Image Rules

1. **Always portrait:** 1024x1536 (fills TikTok screen)
2. **"iPhone photo"** in prompt = looks real, not AI
3. **Lock architecture** across all 6 slides (same room layout)
4. **Include everyday objects** (mugs, remotes) for lived-in feel
5. **Be extremely specific** ("small galley kitchen with white cabinets" not "a kitchen")
